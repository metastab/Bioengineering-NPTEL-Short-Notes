This lecture on **Nucleic Acid & Central Dogma** provides an overview of the fundamental biological molecules and the processes that govern genetic information.

### **1. Nucleic Acids: The Genetic Material**

Nucleic acids are responsible for the **storage and transmission of genetic information**.

  * **Two Classes**: They are classified based on their sugar content into **Deoxyribonucleic Acid (DNA)** and **Ribonucleic Acid (RNA)**.
  * **DNA**: Located in the cell's nucleus, it is the primary constituent of chromosomes and the genetic material.
  * **RNA**: Acts as a "working copy" of DNA and participates in protein synthesis.

#### **Basic Components of Nucleic Acids**

Nucleic acids are built from three main parts:

  * **Four Nitrogenous Bases (Monomers)**:
      * **Pyrimidines** (6-membered rings): Cytosine (C), Thymine (T) in DNA, and Uracil (U) in RNA.
      * **Purines** (double-ring structure): Adenine (A) and Guanine (G).
  * **Pentose Sugar**: Ribose for RNA and deoxyribose for DNA. The main difference is that deoxyribose lacks a hydroxyl (OH) group at the second carbon.
  * **Phosphate Group**: Attached to the C5 hydroxyl of the sugar, this group gives nucleotides a **negative charge**, a property used in DNA electrophoresis.

#### **Structural Terminology**

  * **Nucleoside**: Base + Sugar.
  * **Nucleotide**: Base + Sugar + Phosphate.
  * **Nucleic Acid**: Many nucleotides joined by **phosphodiester bonds**.

### **2. The Structure of DNA**

Watson and Crick deduced the **double helix structure** in 1953, for which they received a Nobel Prize in 1962.

  * **Strands**: Two intertwined anti-parallel strands with a sugar-phosphate backbone on the outside and bases on the inside.
  * **Hydrogen Bonds**: Specific base pairs hold the strands together: **A pairs with T** (2 bonds) and **G pairs with C** (3 bonds).
  * **Replication**: The double helix unwinds at a **replication fork**, allowing each single strand to serve as a template for **DNA polymerase** to add new nucleotides, creating two identical DNA molecules.

### **3. Genes and Operons**

  * **Gene**: A discrete unit of inheritance and a specific DNA sequence that codes for a polypeptide chain or functional RNA molecule.
  * **Eukaryotic Gene Organization**: Includes **exons** (coding regions) and **introns** (non-coding regions) that are removed during RNA processing.
  * **Lac Operon**: A system in bacteria (like *E. coli*) that regulates the metabolism of lactose sugar.
      * **Negative Regulation**: A repressor protein binds to the operator to block transcription unless an inducer (allolactose) inactivates it.
      * **Positive Regulation**: Facilitated by the CAP and cyclic AMP system when glucose levels are low.

### **4. Packaging: Nucleosomes and Histones**

To fit inside the tiny nucleus, DNA is tightly packed.

  * **Histones**: Negatively charged DNA interacts with **positively charged histone proteins** (H2A, H2B, H3, and H4).
  * **Nucleosome**: The structure formed by DNA wrapping around histones, often described as "**beads on a string**".
  * **Chromatin**: A fiber consisting of packed nucleosomes.

### **5. Central Dogma and Omics**

The **Central Dogma** describes the orderly, unidirectional flow of genetic information: **DNA → RNA → Protein**.

  * **Transcription**: The process of making an RNA copy from DNA.
  * **Translation**: The process of using RNA to synthesize proteins.
  * **Diversity of Products**:
      * **Alternative Splicing**: Combining exons in different ways to allow a single gene to produce multiple protein isoforms.
      * **Post-translational Modifications**: Adding chemical groups (like phosphorylation or methylation) to proteins after synthesis to change their function.
  * **Omics**: A field that studies all molecules in a system (genome, transcriptome, proteome) to understand complex networks and advance **personalized medicine**.

#### Short-Answer Solutions

1.  A **nucleoside** is composed of a nitrogenous base and a sugar. A **nucleotide** is formed when a phosphate group is added to a nucleoside (Base + Sugar + Phosphate). **Nucleic acids** are long chains of many nucleotides joined together by phosphodiester bonds.
2.  **Histones** are positively charged proteins that interact with negatively charged DNA molecules. This interaction helps to compact and tightly pack long DNA strands into the small area of the nucleus.
3.  The **Central Dogma** is the orderly and unidirectional flow of genetic information within a cell. It typically moves from **DNA to RNA** through transcription, and then from **RNA to protein** through translation.
4.  The **Human Genome Project** was an ambitious scientific effort to understand the entire human genome by mapping all human genes and governing their functions.
5.  The **Omics** field aims to study all the molecules (genes, transcripts, proteins) in a biological system at once to understand complex signaling networks. This holistic information is used in **personalized medicine** to tailor diagnostic tests and medical treatments to a specific individual’s biomolecular profile.
